VISION STATEMENT FOR THE PRODUCT
The project's goal is to provide a reliable, student-run platform that will enable Walter 
Sisulu University students to choose off-campus housing in a transparent, safe, and 
educated manner. Verified peer reviews, real-time issue reporting, and access to 
objective residence insights will all help achieve this.

THE PROBLEM STATEMENT
There are a number of obstacles for students looking for off-campus housing. These 
include biased suggestions made by SRC members or university representatives, a lack 
of openness about living conditions, and the delayed identification of problems like 
shoddy upkeep, dangerous locations, and untrustworthy landlords. Furthermore, there 
isn't a single place where students may view other students' verified experiences.
These difficulties frequently lead to unhealthy living circumstances, bad housing 
choices, and financial loss.

SUMMARY OF THE PRODUCT
Verified Walter Sisulu University students can use the web-based Student Off-Campus 
Residence Review and Reporting System. Through the system, students may access 
aggregated ratings and insights, submit real-time maintenance or safety complaints, 
exchange organised and honest reviews of residences, and ultimately make data-driven 
housing decisions.
The platform is intended to encourage accountability, openness, and authenticity.

TARGET USERS
Principal Users
Students at Walter Sisulu University who are searching for off-campus housing are the 
system's main users.
Secondary Users
Current renters who want to report problems, prospective students preparing to move, 
and possibly moral landlords who respect openness are examples of secondary users.

CORE FEATURES
Reviews from Verified Students
Reviews can only be posted by enrolled Walter Sisulu University students. 
Infrastructure quality, safety and security, landlord behaviour, maintenance response, 
and transport accessibility are just a few of the themes that will be included in the 
organised framework of the reviews.
Reporting Issues in Real Time
Pupils will be able to record difficulties like electricity or water interruptions. Status 
monitoring will be incorporated into the system to track developments from reported to 
acknowledged to resolved.
Rating System for Residences
Aggregate scores for each residence will be determined by student input. To improve 
comprehension, the system will display visual summaries of ratings.
Look for and Find
Residences will be filtered by users according to factors like price range, location, 
rating, and safety level.
System of Verification
To stop fraudulent or landlord-generated evaluations, a student authentication 
procedure, like using a university email address, will be put in place.
PROPOSITION OF VALUE
For Learners
Students can use the system to make well-informed housing decisions, steer clear of 
dangerous or badly run homes, and have access to actual experiences rather than 
advertisements.
For the Community
The tool helps create a transparent student housing environment and promotes 
accountability among landlords.
Important Distinguishers
Only authenticated reviewers are permitted by the system, which lessens the 
possibility of fraudulent reviews. Compared to generic remarks, it employs a structured 
review format that is more instructive. Instead of concentrating just on prior 
experiences, it incorporates real-time issue reporting. Additionally, it is made especially 
to meet the needs of students in terms of affordability, safety, and transportation.
SUCCESS METRICS
The number of verified student users, the quantity of evaluations produced by each 
house, and the degree of user engagement—including searches, views, and reports—
will all be used to gauge the system's success. Improvements in the calibre and 
thoroughness of evaluations and a decline in complaints regarding off-campus housing 
are examples of long-term success indicators.
VISION FOR THE FUTURE
A mobile application version of the platform, interaction with university housing 
systems, a landlord response portal, AI-based housing recommendations, and 
location-based safety warnings are possible future enhancements.
Elevator Pitch
By sharing personal experiences, reporting problems, and offering clear insights about 
off-campus living, this project gives Walter Sisulu University students a reliable 
platform to assist one another in avoiding bad housing choices.
